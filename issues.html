Neumorphism

Accessibility Mode:

Two main problems

Accessibility

As I mentioned in the overview, by far the biggest issue of Neumorphism is low contrast. Unlike other styles, overall uniformity is one of the key principles. You can slightly improve the situation by manually tweaking the colors, but it still compares badly to usual websites. That is one of the reasons we don't see Neumorphism more often.

If the issue is to be properly addressed, I think introducing an Accessibility Mode is the way to go about it. Such an option solves the problem without any design tradeoffs (which would be detremental to this particular style). However, this basically means adding an additional substyle to your website that would have to be developed alongside the main elements, further weighing down the excessive CSS required for this type of style.

Which brings us to problem number two.

Image by pikisuperstar on Freepik



Complexity

Even though the basis of Neumorphism is incredibly simple, the design overall takes a lot of time, effort, and experience to get right. I am still a novice, but this is not my first time working with CSS. Yet the styling of this website took me easily a dozen hours. Most of the time comes from small tweaks - oftentimes something doesn't feel quite right and fixing it can take absolute ages.

Furthermore, the relatively complex layout and look of elements means that positioning them accurately and accounting for different types of devices is also harder than usual.



Main Point

In conclusion, Neumorphism is an interesting design, but it is not scalable. It would raise small blog or CV page would to a whole new level, but for something more complex like a company website it would become an absolute nightmare to manage and grow.

Luckily, I am guessig that if you are still reading, you are not a big executive of a multi-million corporation, and probably aren't doing a megaproject, so this style might actually be suitable for you.

If that is the case, I have just the thing - a configurable implementation of the very basics of Neumorphism that will allow you to jump past the first few hours of development. Right on the next page!
